Frequently Asked Questions

    1. What is the Sangguniang Kabataan (SK)? The Sangguniang Kabataan (SK) is the youth council in the Philippines that is responsible for implementing youth programs and activities in the barangay. It aims to promote youth participation and leadership at the local level.

    2. Who can become a member of the Sangguniang Kabataan? To become a member of the SK, you must be a Filipino citizen, at least 15 but not older than 30 years old, and a resident of the barangay where you are running for office or participating in SK activities.

    3. How can I get involved with the Sangguniang Kabataan? You can get involved by participating in SK events, volunteering for SK programs, or running for a position in the SK elections. For more details on how to get involved, you can contact your local SK office.

    4. What are the main functions of the Sangguniang Kabataan? The SK is responsible for planning and implementing youth programs and projects, advocating for youth issues, and providing a platform for young people to express their concerns and ideas. They also manage the SK budget and collaborate with other local government units.

    5. How can I contact the Sangguniang Kabataan? You can contact the Sangguniang Kabataan through your local barangay office. For specific inquiries, you can also reach out to us via email or phone. Please visit our Contact Us page for more information.
