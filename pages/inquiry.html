INQUIRY

The Sangguniang Kabataan (SK) is a key element in the local governance system of the Philippines, designed to involve and empower the youth. Established by the Local Government Code of 1991 and reformed by the SK Reform Act of 2015, the SK provides a structured platform for young Filipinos to participate in their communities and local governance.

What is the Sangguniang Kabataan?

The SK is the local youth council in barangays, which are the smallest administrative units in the Philippines. It is composed of elected youth officials, including the SK Chairperson and seven councilors. These officials are elected by youth residents of the barangay aged 15 to 30 years old.

Objectives of the Sangguniang Kabataan

  • Promote Youth Involvement: The SK encourages young people to participate in local governance and decision-making.
  • Address Youth Concerns: It focuses on issues such as education, employment, health, and recreational activities.
  • Develop Leadership Skills: SK involvement helps young leaders improve their skills in leadership, public speaking, and community organizing.
  • Enhance Community Development: SK initiates projects aimed at improving infrastructure, promoting environmental sustainability, and organizing community events.
